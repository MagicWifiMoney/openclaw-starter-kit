# Things You Can Ask Your AI Operator

Copy and paste any of these prompts into your chat. Replace anything in [brackets] with your specific details.

---

## Research

### Research a Competitor
> "Research [competitor name] and tell me their strengths, weaknesses, pricing, and where we have an advantage"

Pulls public info from their website, reviews, news, and social media to give you competitive intelligence.

### Industry Trend Report
> "What are the biggest trends in [your industry] right now? Give me a summary with sources"

Scans recent news, reports, and discussions to identify what's happening in your market.

### Client Background Check
> "Research [company name] before my meeting tomorrow. Tell me about their business, recent news, and key people"

Gathers background information so you walk into meetings prepared and informed.

### Market Analysis
> "Who are the main players in [specific market/niche] and what are they charging?"

Maps out the competitive landscape with pricing, positioning, and market share insights.

### News Monitoring
> "Set up a daily alert for news about [topic, company, or competitor]. Deliver it to me at [time]"

Keeps you informed about specific topics without having to search manually every day.

### Vendor Research
> "I need a [type of tool/service] for [purpose]. Research the top 3 options and compare them"

Evaluates options, compares features and pricing, and recommends the best fit for your needs.

## Writing

### Follow-Up Email
> "Write a follow-up email to [client name] about [topic]. Tone should be [professional/friendly/urgent]"

Drafts a personalized follow-up email based on context you provide.

### Cold Outreach
> "Write a cold email to introduce our [product/service] to [target audience]. Focus on [key benefit]"

Creates compelling outreach that highlights value and encourages responses.

### Proposal Draft
> "Draft a proposal for [client name] to [describe project]. Budget is around [amount], timeline is [duration]"

Generates a structured proposal with scope, timeline, deliverables, and pricing.

### Social Media Post
> "Write 3 LinkedIn posts about [topic]. Make them engaging and include a call to action"

Creates ready-to-post social content that drives engagement with your audience.

### Executive Summary
> "I have a [long document/report]. Summarize the key points in 5 bullet points for my executive team"

Distills lengthy content into concise, actionable takeaways for busy stakeholders.

### Meeting Recap
> "Write a meeting recap email covering [topics discussed]. Include action items and next steps"

Turns meeting notes into a professional summary that keeps everyone aligned.

## Analysis

### Document Review
> "Review this [contract/proposal/report] and tell me what's missing, what's unclear, and what questions I should ask"

Analyzes documents and provides feedback on gaps, risks, and areas needing clarification.

### Compare Options
> "I'm deciding between [option A] and [option B] for [purpose]. Compare them and recommend which one to choose"

Evaluates alternatives based on your criteria and provides a clear recommendation.

### Website Audit
> "Audit [website URL] and tell me what they're doing well and what could be improved"

Reviews a website for messaging, design, user experience, and conversion optimization.

### Data Analysis
> "Here's data from [source]. What are the key insights and what should I do about them?"

Finds patterns, trends, and actionable insights from spreadsheets, reports, or raw data.

### Competitive Positioning
> "Analyze how we compare to [competitor names]. Where do we win and where do we lose?"

Maps your positioning against competitors to identify differentiation opportunities.

## Operations

### Morning Briefing
> "Set up a morning briefing at [time]. Include: my calendar, industry news, urgent emails, and priorities for today"

Starts your day with a personalized briefing that keeps you focused and informed.

### Deadline Tracker
> "Track these deadlines: [list deadlines]. Remind me [X days] before each one and on the day of"

Monitors important dates and sends timely reminders so nothing falls through the cracks.

### Pipeline Review
> "Every Monday at [time], review my sales pipeline and tell me which deals need attention this week"

Provides weekly pipeline analysis to help you prioritize follow-ups and close deals.

### Weekly Recap
> "Every Friday afternoon, summarize what I accomplished this week and what's coming up next week"

Creates a weekly retrospective to track progress and plan ahead.

### Task Automation
> "Every [frequency], check [source] for [information] and notify me if [condition]"

Automates routine monitoring tasks so you only get alerted when action is needed.

## Client Work

### Client Proposal
> "Create a proposal for [client name] to [project description]. Include timeline, deliverables, and pricing"

Builds a complete proposal tailored to the client's needs and your service offering.

### Case Study
> "Write a case study about our work with [client name]. We helped them [outcome]. Make it compelling"

Turns client success stories into marketing assets that demonstrate your value.

### Meeting Preparation
> "I have a meeting with [client name] tomorrow about [topic]. Prepare talking points and questions I should ask"

Generates an agenda and prep materials so you run effective, focused meetings.

### Presentation Outline
> "Create an outline for a presentation to [audience] about [topic]. It should be [X] slides and cover [key points]"

Structures your presentation with a logical flow and key messages for your audience.

### Status Report
> "Write a client status report for [project name]. Cover progress, blockers, and next steps"

Creates professional project updates that keep clients informed and confident.

### Onboarding Materials
> "Create an onboarding guide for new clients of [your service]. Cover what they need to know and what to expect"

Develops client-facing materials that set expectations and reduce onboarding friction.

## Pro Tips

**Be specific:** The more detail you provide, the better the results. Instead of "write an email," say "write a follow-up email to Jane about the proposal we sent last Tuesday, asking if she has questions."

**Give context:** Share relevant backgroundâ€”client history, past conversations, your company voice, industry specifics. The agent uses this to personalize responses.

**Iterate:** If the first result isn't quite right, say "Make it shorter" or "More casual tone" or "Add a section about pricing." The agent refines based on your feedback.

**Combine prompts:** You can ask for multiple things at once: "Research this competitor, then draft an email to my team summarizing what we should know about them."

**Set it and forget it:** Use automation for recurring tasks. Ask once, get results on schedule indefinitely.

**Ask for help:** If you're not sure how to phrase something, just ask: "How should I ask you to do [task]?" The agent will guide you.
